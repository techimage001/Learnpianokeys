How it works

Three scores, not one

01 / NOTES

Did you play the right key?

The obvious one, and the only one most apps measure. Useful, but it is the easiest of the three to fake by memorising where the bars fall.

02 / TIME

Did you play it when you should have?

Graded separately, because right notes at the wrong moment is the single most common thing that makes self-taught playing sound self-taught.

03 / READING

Could you do it from the page?

Switch on read mode and the falling notes disappear, leaving the staff. Anything you play then counts towards your literacy score. Nothing else counts.

Underneath that sit the tools you would expect: wait mode holds the music until your fingers arrive, loop any bar or run of bars, practise one hand while the other plays itself, slow the tempo to a crawl, and show or hide fingering and note names as you outgrow them.

Questions

Before you start

Do I need a real piano?

No. You can play with your computer keys or by clicking the on-screen keyboard, and everything still works, including the scoring. A MIDI keyboard is better because it sends the exact note and the exact moment you pressed it, so the timing score means something. Any digital piano with a USB socket will do.

Why can I not learn current pop songs here?

Because arrangements of songs still in copyright have to be licensed title by title from the publisher, and those licences are what the paid apps are really charging you for. Rather than charge you for that, this site sticks to music that is out of copyright, where we can give you the complete piece rather than a locked preview.

What is the literacy score actually measuring?

Only the notes you played with the falling guide switched off, reading from the staff. It is deliberately harder to move than the other two numbers. If your note accuracy is high and your literacy score is not, you are memorising positions rather than reading music, which is the thing most people discover far too late.

Does it work on a phone?

It runs, and you can tap the keys, but a phone screen is too narrow to show a useful stretch of the keyboard. A tablet propped on the music desk, or a laptop, is a much better experience.

Will it always be free?

The tools on this page are free and there is no trial clock on them. If a paid plan is ever added it will be for new material rather than by switching off something you are already using.

Does my playing get sent anywhere?

No. The scoring, the sound and the notation all run inside your browser. Nothing you play leaves your device.
