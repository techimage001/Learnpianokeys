A minor chord on piano

The A minor chord is made of three notes: A, C, E. Counting up from the root, that is three semitones then four. Play it with your right thumb on A, middle finger on C and little finger on E. The same shape gives you any minor chord from any starting note.

A · C · E

How do you play A minor on the piano?

Put your right thumb on A, your middle finger on C and your little finger on E, then press all three together. Fingers 1, 3 and 5. Keep the hand relaxed and let the arm carry the weight rather than pushing with the fingers.

What is the difference between A minor and A major?

One note. The middle note moves by a single semitone. In A minor the middle note is C; in A major it rises one semitone. That single semitone is the whole difference between the bright sound and the sad one.
