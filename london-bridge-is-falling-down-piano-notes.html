London Bridge Is Falling Down: piano notes for beginners

London Bridge Is Falling Down is in C major and runs to 8 bars. A stepwise tune that never jumps more than one note, so the hand barely moves. There are three levels here: the melody on its own, the melody with one bass note per bar, and both hands together. Every note shows the finger to use, and the music waits for you rather than running ahead.

What notes are in London Bridge Is Falling Down?

The melody uses 6 different notes: C, D, E, F, G, A. It is written in C major in 4/4 time, and runs to 8 bars.

Before you start

Common mistakes on London Bridge Is Falling Down

  1. Cutting the two-beat notes short at the end of each line.
  2. Playing the D in bar three with the wrong finger and running out of hand.
  3. Rushing. It is a walking pace, not a march.

The practice room highlights the bars you keep fluffing and offers to loop them, so you do not have to spot these yourself.
