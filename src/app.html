How the practice room works

Six things, and then you can leave this panel alone. If you have never touched a piano, do the five minute walkthrough first: it is gentler than this page.

  1. Pick a piece. Use the dropdown at the top right. Twinkle, Twinkle is the easiest. Start there if you are unsure.
  2. Press Play. You get two bars of clicks to count you in, then the coloured blocks start falling towards the keyboard at the bottom.
  3. Play a block when it lands on the strike line. The colour tells you which hand: blue for your left hand, rose for your right. That never changes, whatever you play. The small number on each block is the finger to use, with 1 as the thumb. A green outline appears once you have played a block in time, and a red one if it went past you.
  4. Nothing runs away from you. Wait mode is on, so the music stops and waits until you play the right note. Drag the Tempo slider down if it still feels fast.
  5. Loop the hard bit. Tap any numbered bar in the Loop a section panel and it repeats until you leave it. Trouble spots also lists the bars you keep fluffing, with a one-tap loop.
  6. Watch three scores, not one. Right notes, in time, and reading. Read mode hides the falling blocks and leaves the stave, and only what you play then counts towards reading. If the stave is still a mystery, the reading guide is free and takes about ten minutes a day.

No keyboard? Tap the on-screen keys, or use your computer's A to K row. A MIDI keyboard plugged in by USB works straight away.

Everything here is free. Sign up with an email whenever you like and the practice room stays unlocked on this device. Nothing is charged and no card details are ever requested.

Practice tools

Wait mode
The music stops and waits until you play the right note. Leave this on until you can keep up.
Read mode
Hides the falling blocks so you play from the written music instead. Harder. Only this counts towards your reading score.
Count in
Two bars of clicks before the music starts, so you are not caught out.
Metronome
A click on every beat while you play, to keep you steady.
Hand letters
Puts an L or an R on every block so you never have to remember which colour is which hand.
Fingering
Puts a number on every block: 1 is your thumb, 5 is your little finger.
Note names
Prints C, D, E and so on along the keys.
Play other hand
When you practise one hand, the app plays the other one for you.
Sustain pedal
Holds the notes ringing. Hold Shift, or use the real pedal on your MIDI keyboard.
Use microphone
For an acoustic piano with no cable. It follows one note at a time, so it is less accurate than plugging in.

Your last run

Hearing your own timing back is the fastest way to fix it. Your playing is replayed from your device only; nothing is uploaded. Everything stays inside the app: nothing is downloaded, and your place is kept for you so you can stop and carry on later.
